## Activation at TGE

Validator nodes are provisioned and activated at the Token Generation Event (TGE). Before TGE, your node license exists as an ERC-721 NFT on Arbitrum. At and after TGE:

1. The Autheo Chain mainnet launches
2. You bridge your license NFT from Arbitrum to Autheo Chain
3. You bind your license and set up your node
4. Your validator enters the active set

## Activation paths

### Self-hosted nodes

1. Receive setup documentation and binary when published before TGE
2. Provision infrastructure meeting the [hardware requirements](/nodes/node-setup/node-setup)
3. Follow the [installation](/nodes/node-setup/installation) and [configuration](/nodes/node-setup/configuration) guides
4. Bridge and [set up your license](/nodes/node-setup/license-setup)
5. [Register your validator](/nodes/validator-lifecycle/register-validator)

### Managed ("1-click") nodes

1. Connect your wallet to the managed hosting platform during onboarding
2. Select your preferred provider (InfStones, Zeeve) and region
3. The provider provisions and activates your node at TGE
4. You retain control of your keys and license

See [Hosting options](/validator-program/hosting-options) for more details on managed providers.

## Testnet participation

Before mainnet launch, Autheo operates a public testnet. Running a testnet node is recommended to:

- Familiarize yourself with `autheod` operations
- Test your infrastructure and monitoring setup
- Validate your license bridging workflow

Follow the testnet guides in the [Nodes section](/nodes/overview) to get started.
